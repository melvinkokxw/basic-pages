Robert Baden-Powell

The father of Scouting

Here's a time line of Lord Baden-Powell's life:

  • 1857 - Born in London
  • 1876 - Joined the 13th Hussars in India with the rank of lieutenant
  • 1884 - Published Reconnaissance and Scouting
  • 1896 - Returned to Africa to serve in the Second Matabele War. A formative experience to develop his Boy Scout ideals later
  • 1897 - Brevetted colonel at the age of 40 (the youngest colonel in the British Army)
  • 1901 - Invested as Companion of the Order of the Bath (CB), the fourth-most senior of the British Orders of Chivalry
  • 1907 - Held a camp on Brownsea Island to test out his ideas on Scouting. The location eventually became known as Brownsea Island Scout camp
  • 1908 - Published the first book on the Scout Movement, Scouting for Boys
  • 1910 - Scouting Movement was started
  • 1912 - Married Olave St Clair Soames, the first Chief Guide for Britain.
  • 1941 - Died on 8 January, buried at St Peter's Cemetery in Nyeri. His gravestone bears a circle with a dot in the centre "Ê˜", which is the trail sign for "Going home", or "I have gone home".

"Try and leave this world a little better than you found it and when your turn comes to die, you can die happy in feeling that at any rate you have not wasted your time but have done your best." -BP
